Modulus of a complex number

In mathematics , the modulus of a complex number is a real number positive that measures its "size" and generalizes the absolute value of a real number. This concept is useful to define a distance on the complex plane .

The modulus of a complex number z is denoted | z |. In Cartesian coordinates , z is equal to a + i b where i is the imaginary unit , a is the real part of z and b the imaginary part , this module is the square root of the sum of squares of a and b :

The term Module was introduced by Jean-Robert Argand , exposing a way of representing imaginary quantities by geometric constructions [1] .
